Short Bio

I am a master student of School of Computer Science at Fudan Univeristy, under the supervision of Professor Yun Xiong. Previously, I received my bachelor’s degree from East China Normal University majored in Computer Science in 2014.

My current research mainly focuses on graph mining and graph representation learning. I want to explore the complex social network and identify useful information from it, so I chose this topic as the starting point for my research at the beginning of my master’s degree.

Publications

  • Yizhu Jiao, Yun Xiong, Jiawei Zhang, Yao Zhang, Tianqi Zhang and Yangyong Zhu. Sub-graph Contrast for Scalable Self-Supervised Graph Representation Learning. ICDM. 2020. [paper] [slide]

    Abstract: A novel self-supervised representation learning method via Sub-graph Contrast is proposed by utilizing the strong correlation between central nodes and their sampled subgraphs to capture regional structure information. It has prominent performance advantages in weaker supervision requirements, model learning scalability, and parallelization.

  • Yizhu Jiao, Yun Xiong, Jiawei Zhang and Yangyong Zhu. Collective Link Prediction Oriented Network Embedding with Hierarchical Graph Attention. CIKM. 2019. [paper] [slide]

    Abstract: This paper proposes an application oriented network embedding framework, Hierarchical Graph Attention based Network Embedding to predict both the intra-network social links and the inter-network anchor links. It learns the node representations by aggregating information from both the intra-network neighbors and inter-network partners.

  • Tianqi Zhang, Yun Xiong, Jiawei Zhang, Yao Zhang, Yizhu Jiao and Yangyong Zhu. CommDGI: Community Detection Oriented Deep Graph Infomax. CIKM. 2020. [paper]

    Abstract: This paper proposes a community oriented node representation learning framework for community detection, which learns the community partition in an end-to-end manner.
